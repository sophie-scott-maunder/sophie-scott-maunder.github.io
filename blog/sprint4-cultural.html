Neuroplasticity

Accepting the principles of neuroplasticity is perhaps the first stage of learning to think in a growth mindset. The principles of neuroplasticity benefit not only those with learning difficulties or those who have suffered from neurological events, but also people who are wanting to learn new skills or improve existing ones.

The principles of neuroplasticity wouldn’t necessarily be new concepts to most people. By engaging and utilising the simple principles of neuroplasticity we can enhance our ability to learn new things. Using, improving, repeating, challenging and training the brain are all important ways to change our own neuroplasticity. I’m interested in how engaging with meaningful and salient learning will change and strengthen the way I retain information.

There are many physical, emotional, intellectual and behavioural ways to increase ones neuroplasticity. It could be as simple as walking home a different way from work than you normally would or it could be practicing mindfulness and meditation in order to awaken your consciousness. Awareness of improving neuroplasticity allows the brain to behave in a growth mindset.

Growth Mindset

A growth mindset is a belief that skills and intelligence are grown and developed through determination and hardwork. It creates the foundation for great learning and shows that it can improve our ability to grow and develop neuroplasticity. Our brains are still capable of learning new things which means that utilising a growth mindset is just as relevant to all areas of our life as it was when we were children.

Since childhood, I’ve generally always operated with a growth mindset (subconsciously or not) and began learning about it this year when I was helping my friend through her teachers college study. I had been under the impression that a growth mindset tends to only be relevant to school children and teenagers so it’s interesting to learn that a lot of corporate companies and organisations are implementing the growth mindset into their business strategies as a way to promote company profit.

Learning a new skill comes with a whole new set of challenges. Finding ways of strategising and overcoming these challenges is going to be key to achieving successes throughout my learning journey. Things to personally focus on will be language which promotes a growth mindset. Instead of focusing on the end destination I’m going to focus on the journey of learning.

Learning Plan and Strategies

  • Perfectionism - whatever you do is good enough for now, come back and work on it later
  • Complete the easiest things first - seeing progress creates more progress
  • Break down hard things into smaller steps
  • Temptations add fuel to the fire - celebrate milestones
  • Find the flow - find when the creative flow is “just right” and keep working through it
